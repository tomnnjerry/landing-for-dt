The Origin

A vision for our people.

Tourism has always been the lifeblood of our hills. Growing up in Siliguri, I saw the beauty of this industry, but I also saw its quiet struggles.

For years, I watched the hardest workers—the local drivers navigating dangerous mist, the guides sharing their culture, and the homestay owners cooking warm meals—being heavily underpaid. Big agencies would take the lion's share, leaving the true hosts of the Himalayas struggling to support their own families.

I realized that if we truly love these mountains, we have to respect the people who make them feel like home. That is why Himalayan Hives was born. We removed the middlemen to create a fair, honest ecosystem where local people are paid what they deserve, and travelers get genuine, wholehearted hospitality.

Manish Giri
Founder, Himalayan Hives

Beyond Tourism

Leaving a gentle footprint.

When you visit our home, you help us keep it beautiful and thriving.

Tourism can sometimes take more from a place than it gives back. At Himalayan Hives, we are determined to do things differently. We believe that every trip should leave behind a positive ripple in the communities you visit.

By choosing to travel with a locally-owned company, your money doesn't disappear into a corporate headquarters in a big city. It stays right here in the hills. It pays for a local driver's daughter to attend school, helps a homestay mother repair her roof before the monsoons, and supports the farmers who grow your morning tea.

Empowering Homestays

We prioritize family-run homestays, especially those led by women. This brings a direct, reliable income to rural households that often get bypassed by big hotel chains.

Micro-Entrepreneurs

Our drivers are not underpaid employees. We help them own and maintain their own vehicles, turning them into proud, independent business owners.

Silent Trails

We educate our guests on how to respect sacred spaces. We carry our own trash, use zero single-use plastics in our cars, and always leave the hills exactly as we found them.

A Shared Success

You get the trip of a lifetime, and the local community gets a fair, honest living. It is a beautiful exchange of cultures, built entirely on mutual respect.

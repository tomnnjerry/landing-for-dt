Day 05

The High Ridge Walk.

Today we leave the car behind. We walk up to a high ridge to get closer to the big peaks. It's quiet, clean, and very beautiful.

Pack Light

You only need your camera and water for the walk. We handle the heavy bags. Wear your best walking shoes today.

The Morning Ascent

We drive 45 minutes to the start of the trail. The walk up takes about 3 to 4 hours. It is an uphill path through local villages and forests. You will see the local life away from the city.

The "Big Three" View

When we reach the ridge, the view opens up. You see Machhapuchhre (Fishtail), Annapurna, and Dhaulagiri right in front of you. There are no crowds here—just the wind and the mountains.

The Local Stay

We stay the night in a simple, clean local lodge on the ridge. The rooms are basic but the view from the window is better than any 5-star hotel. We have hot local tea and dinner by the fire.

Honest Intelligence

Myth vs. Reality

The Myth

“It’s just a short drive from the border to Kathmandu. We can take a bus to save money.”

The Real

The road is narrow, twisty, and full of trucks. A bus can take 15 hours. We fly you from Bhadrapur in 45 minutes so you don't waste your first two days being tired.

The Myth

“Indian ₹500 and ₹200 notes are illegal in Nepal. I should only carry small change.”

The Real

As of 2026, ₹500 notes are legal for Indian tourists up to a limit. However, ₹100 notes are still the "Local King"—they are accepted everywhere without questions or bad exchange rates.

The Myth

“Nepal food is exactly like North Indian food. I will just get Daal-Roti everywhere.”

The Real

Nepal has its own soul. You haven't tasted Nepal until you try Buff Momos (Buffalo meat) and Newari Khaja sets. The spices are different, and the Bubble Tea in Thamel is a must.

The Myth

“Trekking is only for athletes. I am not fit enough to see the big mountains.”

The Real

Our 1-Day Ridge Walk is built for regular people. If you can walk for 3 hours, you can do it. You don't need to be a climber to stand in front of the Annapurna peaks.

The Field Guide

Weather Guide

Spring (March - May) Best for flowers & warm walks. Clear mornings.
Autumn (Sept - Nov) The sharpest mountain views. 10/10 visibility.
Winter (Dec - Feb) Cold but zero crowds. Snowy peaks.
Summer (June - Aug) Rainy season. Lush green but clouds hide peaks.

Road Guide

We use the B.P. Highway for all ground travel. It is a Japan-built masterpiece—short, scenic, and safe.

The "Old Route" takes 16 hours and is full of heavy trucks. We avoid this completely to keep you fresh and safe.

The Truth about the Road

While the B.P. Highway is beautiful, it is winding. If you get motion sickness, we recommend taking a light tablet before the drive. This is why we prefer flying—it's 45 mins vs 10 hours of curves.

Important Places.

Thamel, Kathmandu

The main hub for travelers. It is full of small shops, local gear, and the best cafes in the city. It is loud, busy, and the best place to feel the energy of the city.

Pro Tip: Best place for evening live music and gear shopping.

Boudhanath Stupa

The massive white dome in the city center. It is a very peaceful place where people walk in circles to pray. The eyes of Buddha look out over the whole area.

Pro Tip: Go to the rooftops for coffee and a clear photo of the dome.

Pashupatinath Temple

The most sacred Hindu site in Nepal. It sits on the banks of the river. It is a deep and spiritual place where you see the real heart of local faith.

Pro Tip: Watch the evening Aarti lights from across the river bank.

Kathmandu Durbar Square

The old palace area. You will see ancient wood carvings that are hundreds of years old. It is the best place to see how kings once lived in Kathmandu.

Pro Tip: Look for the Kumari (Living Goddess) at her window.

Phewa Lake, Pokhara

The soul of Pokhara. A giant, quiet lake with the high mountains in the background. It is much cleaner and slower than the capital city.

Pro Tip: Take a wooden boat out early in the morning for zero noise.

The High Ridge

Our favorite spot for mountain views. From here, you see the "Big Three" peaks right in front of you. It is the most beautiful sunrise you will ever see.

Pro Tip: Wake up 30 mins before sunrise. The "Pink light" is the best.

Border Compliance

Paperwork Sorted.

No Visa Required

For Indian citizens, there is no visa needed to enter Nepal. You can stay as long as you like for your holiday. We handle the entry forms at the Kakarbhitta border for you.

Carry Photos

Bring 04 to 06 passport-size photos with a white background. You will need these for your local SIM card, trekking permits, and some heritage site entry forms.

Original Documents

Digital copies or photocopies are not enough. You must carry the physical original cards with you. Our team will help you keep them safe during the trip.

SIM Registration

You need your ID copy and one photo to get a local Ncell or Nepal Telecom SIM. We help you get this done in the first 10 minutes after crossing the border.

The Local Pro Truth: ID Selection

The **Voter ID Card** or **Passport** are the only IDs accepted at the airport for Indian citizens. **Aadhaar Cards are often rejected at airports.** If you are flying with us, please bring your Original Voter ID or Passport.

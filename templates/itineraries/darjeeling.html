The Darjeeling
Field Guide.

Climate Windows

Spring (Mar - May) Peak bloom. Rhododendrons and clear Kanchenjunga mornings.
Autumn (Oct - Nov) The "Golden Window." Sharpest visibility for Tiger Hill sunrise.
Winter (Dec - Feb) Very cold, but no crowds. Crystal blue skies and sunset fire.
Monsoon (June - Sept) The "Deep Green" phase. Mist and rain. Peaks are hidden but tea gardens glow.

The international twist in our loop. Located right on the India-Nepal border. You get to walk across into Nepal for an hour of electronics and gear shopping at local rates.

A massive natural lake with a 240ft arched bridge. The descent via Mirik is the smoothest road in the hills, passing through the beautiful orange orchards of Dudhia.

Darjeeling Chowrasta

The social heart of the town. No cars allowed. It's a place for long walks, old bookstores, and the heritage Glenary's bakery. Perfect for people-watching and evening mists.

Local Tip: Visit the hidden lane behind the horse-stable for local snacks.
